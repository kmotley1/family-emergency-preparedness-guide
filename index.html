Straight Answers

Is this guide political?
No. Preparedness doesn't have a party affiliation. The guide draws from experiences of real political upheaval and focuses on practical action that protects any family regardless of their politics. Hurricanes, power outages, and economic downturns don't check voter registration.
Do I need to be technical to use the Obsidian vault?
Basic familiarity with Obsidian helps, but we include a quick-start instruction file. If you've never used Obsidian, you can download it free and be up and running in 15 minutes. Alternatively, just use the PDF — it has everything.
Is this just a list of stuff to buy?
Definitely not. Less than 20% of the guide is about physical supplies. The rest covers skills, plans, community, financial resilience, and mindset. You can start today with zero dollars spent.
I live in an apartment. Is this relevant to me?
Yes. Every section includes apartment-appropriate options — container gardening instead of a yard, go-bags that fit under a bed, community networks with neighbors. The principles work regardless of your living situation.
How is this different from free FEMA guides?
FEMA guides are written by committees for the broadest possible audience. This guide is written from personal experience with actual political and economic instability — and it doesn't pull punches about what really matters when systems fail.
Will I get future updates?
Yes. The guide is actively being expanded. As new content, checklists, and resources are added, anyone who's purchased will receive the updated versions at no additional cost.
